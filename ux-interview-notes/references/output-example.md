### Post-session rapid synthesis

| | |
|---|---|
| **Primary problem being solved** | Staff receipt chain: WhatsApp group to screenshot to email to bookkeeper. Four manual steps that fall behind on busy weeks. |
| **Most compelling value** | Mobile photo upload that staff can use without a Xero account or login. |
| **Biggest trust barrier** | Staff adoption, not Jordan's own adoption. Jordan will set it up; the question is whether staff will use it consistently. |
| **Conditions for adoption** | Staff upload requires no Xero account and no more friction than WhatsApp. Scoped camera roll access. Human review before Xero posting. |
| **Conditions for switching from Xero** | Low loyalty. Mobile receipt capture would be a switching trigger. |

**Notable signal: the WhatsApp group as a design benchmark.** Jordan described WhatsApp as the standard that any alternative must match or beat. It requires no login, no app install beyond what staff already have, and one tap to share. Any staff-facing receipt capture UX that adds more steps than this will fail at adoption, regardless of how clean the back-end is.

**Notable signal: the Bunnings gap was unknown.** Jordan assumed Bunnings PowerPass invoices were reaching the bookkeeper. They were not. This is a live $1,200/month gap in the expense record that Jordan had no visibility into. The auto data retrieval concept resolved it — but the more important insight is that gaps like this exist without the business owner knowing. Discovery of an invisible gap is a more compelling value proposition than convenience.

### So what / Unresolved questions

#### Design: So what?

| Question | Why it matters from this session |
|---|---|
| What is the minimum interaction required for a staff member to submit a receipt on mobile without a Xero account? | Jordan's adoption condition is friction parity with WhatsApp. The design question is whether a share-sheet extension, a QR code link, or an SMS/email-to-inbox model best meets that bar. |
| Where does the human review step live in the flow, and what information does it need to show for Jordan to approve without cross-referencing Xero? | Jordan said "I don't want it just deciding things" but also doesn't want to add a step to their current workflow. The review step needs to fit into an existing habit, not create a new one. |
| How should the Bunnings PowerPass connection be surfaced during onboarding — as a discovery prompt or as part of a manual connection flow? | Jordan didn't know the gap existed. A passive onboarding prompt ("we found a Bunnings PowerPass account associated with your email — connect it?") may be more effective than expecting the user to seek out the connection. |

#### Product: So what?

| Question | Why it matters from this session |
|---|---|
| Is the "staff submits receipts without a Xero account" use case common enough across the trades and services segment to justify a dedicated staff-capture UX, or is it better served by a shared link / guest upload model? | Jordan has 8 staff who would never have their own Xero login. This is likely common across landscaping, cleaning, construction, and other field-based service businesses. The product question is whether to build a proper staff role or a lighter guest-submission flow. |
| Does auto-discovery of connected-but-not-reconciled supplier accounts (like Bunnings PowerPass) belong in onboarding, or as an ongoing alert for existing users? | Jordan's Bunnings gap had existed for an unknown period. New user onboarding is the obvious entry point, but existing users with similar silent gaps may be the larger opportunity. |

#### Engineering / Dev: So what?

| Question | Why it matters from this session |
|---|---|
| What does a guest or staff upload flow look like technically if the submitter has no Xero account — token-based link, SMS trigger, or something else? | Staff adoption depends on zero-login friction. The technical architecture of how a receipt gets from a staff member's phone to Xero without requiring account creation determines whether this use case is deliverable at all. |
| Does Bunnings PowerPass expose invoice data via API, or does the existing MYOB/Xero integration rely on email parsing? | If PowerPass uses email-based delivery, the auto data retrieval concept works today. If it requires a platform API, the integration complexity is higher and the roadmap question is whether to build it or rely on email as a fallback. |
